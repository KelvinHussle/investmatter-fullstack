Frequently Asked Questions

Question: How can I invest in Tanzania?

Answer:

Direct investment: You can set up your own business in Tanzania or invest in an existing Tanzanian business.

Portfolio investment: You can invest in Tanzanian stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments.

Indirect investment: You can invest in Tanzania through mutual funds or other investment vehicles that invest in Tanzanian assets.

Question: What are the tax implications for foreign investors in Tanzania?

Answer:

According to TRA in 2023, Foreign investors in Tanzania are subject to the same taxes as Tanzanian citizens. The corporate tax rate in Tanzania is 30%. There is also a value-added tax (VAT) of 18%.

Question: What are the sectors that are open to foreign investment?

Answer:

Agriculture: Agriculture is the backbone of the Tanzanian economy and employs over 70% of the population. There are opportunities for investment in the entire agricultural value chain, from production to processing to marketing.

Tourism: Tanzania is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, wildlife, and national parks. There are opportunities for investment in the tourism sector, such as hotels, lodges, and tour operators.

Mining: Tanzania is rich in mineral resources, including gold, copper, and nickel. There are opportunities for investment in all aspects of the mining sector, from exploration to production to processing.

Question: What are the benefits of investing in Tanzania?

Answer:

Access to a growing market: Tanzania has a population of over 60 million people, which is expected to reach 100 million by 2050. This provides a large and growing market for goods and services.

Abundant natural resources: Tanzania is rich in natural resources, including minerals, land, and wildlife. This provides opportunities for investment in a wide range of sectors, such as mining, agriculture, and tourism.

Favorable government policies: The Tanzanian government is committed to attracting foreign investment and has made significant reforms to the investment climate in recent years. These reforms include streamlining the business registration process, reducing taxes, and providing incentives for foreign investors.

Question: Where can I get more information about investing in Tanzania?

Answer:

The Tanzania Investment Centre (TIC) is the government agency responsible for promoting and attracting foreign investment to Tanzania. The TIC website provides information on the investment climate in Tanzania, the investment process, and investment opportunities.
